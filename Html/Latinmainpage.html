The History of Latin

Latin dance was created from Mexican, South American, Central American and Caribbean culture and was performed by the native people of Latin America. Due to the European colonization, latin dance has been greatly influenced by Europeans and by African slaves. The dances were very specific and organized. They also represented many events such as the sowing and harvesting movement. Modern latin dance was developed across many countries, some places are Cuba, Puerto Rico, Brazil and Spain. Some dances created from these places are the Bolero, the Cha Cha, Rumba, Mambo, Merengue, the Paso Doble and the Samba. All of these dances and more were altered and taken in by the United States and Europe.

Famous Latin Dancers
Yulia Zagoruychenko
Yulia Zagoruychenko is originally from Russia, and she is an eight time Latin Dancer champion. Zagoruychenko started dancing at the age of 7, and would copy the people she saw on television. She started by taking folk dancing classes, and then she switched to Ballroom dance. Zagoruychenko start teaching kids to dance at the age of 11, to earn extra money. Riccardo Cocchi
Riccardo Cocchi is a Latin Dancer, and he was born in Terni, Italy. He is a eight- time champion, with his Yulia Zagoruychenko. Cocchi, started dancing at the age of 6, in his parent's dance studio. In his early age, he would compete in various dance competitions, with different partners, and he won many titles since as, "World Amauter Latin Champion". He continued to dance in his adulthood, and he partnered with Zagoruychenko. They won their first Latin Dance championship in 2010, and won their second one in 2011. Riccardo and Zagoruychenko are ranked number number one for Professional Latin Dance. Cocchi is still dancing, and he is also coaching people who want to become better Latin Dancers.
