The History of Hip-Hop

Hip Hop dancing emerged in the late 1960’s to 1970’s in the Bronx, New York. This kind of dancing happened on the streets by people who didn’t professionally dance or people who didn’t attend dance academies. DJ’s would mix records that matched well with the break dancing of this time. Soon after, hip hop dancing spread from the east coast to the west coast. The West coast adopting hip hop dancing and making it their own. They created locking, popping, and krumping, which eventually was encompassed by the East Coast. As the 80’s came near breakdancing began to fade and more expressive types of hip hop dancing became popular. Which was influenced by the rise of freestyle rap and the club scene. The two different hip hop styles eventually emerged, and people continue to add their own creativity into new dances.

Famous Hip Hop Dancers
Teyana Taylor
Teyana Taylor is a rapper, dancer, actress, singer, and songwriter from Harlem. Teyana started by performing in local talent competitions, like the Apollo Talent Search. In Teyana’s career she has made the "Chicken Noodle Soup" dance popular, and she also was apart of the choreography for Beyoncè’s "Ring the Alarm" video. She signed with Pharrell’s label, then she switched to Kanye’s Label in 2012. She has starred in some popular dance movies, such as "Stomp the Yard 2:Homecoming ", and her most recent appearance in the movie, "Honey: Rise Up and Dance ",. Teyana also has her own dance workout dvds, called Fade to Fit.
Chris Brown
Chris Brown is a music, and dance entertainer, who is also a Grammy Award winner. He was born, and raised in Tappahannock, Virginia. Chris started off singing in Church, and he also would do dances initiating some popular artist. He was discovered by a lady named, Tina Davis, and was signed with Jive Records. During his career he was often compared to, Michael Jackson, for his dancing and singing abilities. Chris went on to doing movies, where he used his dancing skills to be apart of the movie, "Stomp the Yard". Chris, then goes to do a Michael Jackson tribute, at the 2010 "Bet Awards", after the death of the legend. Chris is still continuing his career in music, recently releasing his album, "HeartBreak on a Full Moon", at the end of 2017.
