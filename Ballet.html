Ballet Starter Positions

First Position

Place your heels touching together and your feet facing in opposite directions. Make sure your legs are turned out right from your thighs, not just below your knees or you could get serious knee injuries.

Second Position

 This is like first position, but place your feet about a foot apart. Keep your toes turned outwards.

Third Position

 Third position is rarely used anymore, because with the high degree of turnout of today's dancers, it looks too similar to Fifth Position.

Fourth Position

Place your feet turned outwards, but place your front foot about six inches in front of your back foot. This can be done from first-open fourth; or from third; crossed fourth.

Fifth Position

Fifth is similar to first, but you place your front foot's heel at your back foot's toe.
